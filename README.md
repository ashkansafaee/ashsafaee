## Thesis

**[Grokking through the Lens of Information Theory](./Grokking_through_the_lens_of_information_theory.pdf)** — B.Sc. Thesis, Grade A
Supervised by Ola Hössjer & Leo Levenius

Why do neural networks sometimes keep getting better long after they've already mastered their training data — as if something suddenly clicks into place? This thesis connects two information-theoretic frameworks (Tishby's information bottleneck and Clauw et al.'s synergy/redundancy decomposition) to argue that "grokking" is what compression looks like from the inside: a network shedding memorised noise in favour of a generalising, compressed representation — with weight decay as the mechanism that pushes it there.
